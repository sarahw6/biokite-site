Market

The market nobody built for.

100,000 rural clinics across sub-Saharan Africa lack reliable power. Over a billion people depend on them. The grid is not coming. Solar-plus-storage is proven. What is missing is a deployment model that works within existing health systems and stays after the NGO leaves.

100,000

Rural clinics across Africa without reliable power. Each serves an average of 10,000 patients per year. BioKite targets dispensaries and health centres where backup power does not exist.

$300M–$500M

Estimated serviceable addressable market across East and West Africa. Per-clinic costs of $3,000–$5,000 including hardware, installation, training, and first-year monitoring.

Revenue Model

Three revenue streams. One mission.

Stream 1

Government Contracts

County and national health authorities contract BioKite to deploy Clinic Ready systems across their facilities. Per-clinic pricing. Scalable across counties and countries.

Stream 2

Corporate ESG Fees

Companies pay per battery diverted. They receive auditable impact data for sustainability reports. Battery diversion volume, CO2 offset, and health outcomes tracked quarterly.

Stream 3

Certification Licensing

The Certified Energy Officer program and Clinical Readiness Protocol can be licensed to other organizations deploying in new regions. Training materials, exam standards, and scorecard systems included.

Cost-Effectiveness

1/21 the WHO cost-effectiveness threshold.

The World Health Organization considers an intervention "highly cost-effective" when it costs less than the GDP per capita of the country per DALY averted. BioKite's estimate of <$105 per life-year saved is 1/21 of that threshold. The methodology is available.
