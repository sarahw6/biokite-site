Why This Exists

An infrastructure of survival

Someone I loved nearly died because the systems meant to keep her alive were fragile. Machines failed, power failed, coordination failed. In those hours it became painfully clear how much survival depends not just on medicine, but on the invisible infrastructure around it — electricity, reliability, time.

That experience changed the course of my life.

BioKite Labs exists to solve a problem that is both enormous and strangely ignored: the absence of reliable power in rural health clinics.

The Problem

When power fails, the margin between life and death narrows to chance

Nearly a billion people live with electricity that disappears several times a week, often for hours. When it does, the consequences inside a health clinic are immediate and severe.

Oxygen Stops Flowing

Oxygen concentrators require continuous power. When the grid fails, patients on supplemental oxygen are left without it — sometimes for hours.

Vaccines Spoil

Cold chain breaks destroy temperature-sensitive vaccines and reagents. Once a cold chain is broken, those supplies cannot be recovered.

Surgical Lights Go Dark

Procedures underway when the power drops leave clinicians operating by phone flashlight — or stopping entirely mid-intervention.

Survival Becomes Chance

Without reliable power, clinical outcomes depend on when the grid happens to be working — not on the quality of care a clinic can provide.

Our Approach

Simple by design, sovereign by intent

The approach is simple: power is reserved first and permanently for the equipment that saves lives. Everything else requires deliberate activation.

Life-Saving Equipment First

Power is reserved permanently for oxygen concentrators, vaccine refrigerators, surgical lights, and diagnostic equipment. These never compete with general loads.

Clinic-Owned Infrastructure

The system is owned by the clinic itself. No contracts, no subscriptions, no external dependencies that can be withdrawn.

Certified Energy Officers

Each system is maintained by a trained energy officer at the facility. Local expertise, local accountability, no reliance on distant technicians.

Deliberate Activation

Non-critical loads require deliberate activation — ensuring that the power reserved for patients is never accidentally consumed by administrative or comfort loads.

This is not a product that gets installed and forgotten. It is a shift in how a clinic relates to its own infrastructure — from dependency to control, from fragility to resilience.
The system is designed to be maintained without contracts or subscriptions. Every component can be serviced locally, replaced affordably, and understood by the people who rely on it.
Where We Begin

Starting in Kenya

Our work begins in Kenya, where we are piloting systems with county health authorities to ensure clinics can continue functioning when the grid fails.

We are working directly with county health teams to understand the specific power challenges each facility faces — and to build systems that fit within existing maintenance and governance structures.

The Goal

The goal is not charity. It is sovereignty.

"A clinic that controls its own power controls its ability to care for the people who depend on it."

We believe that reliable power is not a luxury or an add-on. It is the foundation on which every other intervention — medication, surgery, diagnostics, vaccination — depends. Without it, healthcare is a promise that cannot be kept.
