For Health Directors

Keep your clinic powered during outages.
Owned by your community. No subscriptions.

BioKite installs facility-owned backup power, trains a certified energy officer from your community, and gives you a weekly scorecard proving your clinic is ready.

What We Install

Equipment that stays with your facility. Permanently.

⚡

LiFePO4 Battery System

Built from tested, retired EV batteries. Powers oxygen concentrators, surgical lights, and delivery room equipment through outages. Your facility owns it outright.

🌡

Cold Chain Monitor

Continuous temperature and voltage tracking for vaccine storage. Every degree logged. Every outage timestamped. Works entirely offline. Audit-ready.

☼

Solar Charging Array

Grid-independent recharging. The battery refills during the day so it is ready when the grid fails at night. No fuel costs. No generator dependency.

Who We Train

A Certified Energy Officer from your community.

Not an outside contractor. Someone who knows the patients by name. Trained through a 160-hour certification covering battery systems, cold chain integrity, emergency response, and weekly reporting. They stay because they belong there.

Training Includes

  • Battery assembly, installation, and maintenance
  • Cold chain monitoring and compliance
  • Emergency response protocols
  • Weekly scorecard reporting

Certification Outcome

  • Hands-on field exam (pass/fail)
  • Credential recognized by county health authorities
  • Annual recertification requirement
  • Accountable to your facility, not to us
Accountability

The weekly scorecard.

Every week, your Certified Energy Officer submits a scorecard. You see exactly what happened, what was handled, and what needs attention. No gaps. No guesswork.

Timeline

From first contact to Clinic Ready.

1
Week 1–2

Intake & site assessment

We visit your facility, assess power needs, map critical equipment, and confirm feasibility. You tell us what matters most. We listen.

2
Week 3–6

Officer selection & training begins

A community member is selected and begins the 160-hour certification program. They train on your equipment, at your facility.

3
Week 6–8

Equipment installation

Battery system, solar array, and cold chain monitor installed. Wiring tested. Critical loads connected. Your facility owns every component.

4
Week 8–10

Certification exam & go-live

Officer completes hands-on field exam. System goes live. First weekly scorecard submitted. Your clinic is certified Clinic Ready.
